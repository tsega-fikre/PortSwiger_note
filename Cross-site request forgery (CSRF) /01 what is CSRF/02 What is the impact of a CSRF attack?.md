# What is the impact of a CSRF attack?

You’re still logged into your bank, and a hacker tricks your browser into sending a money transfer request. Here’s what could happen next:

- **Money stolen** – The hacker sends money from your account to theirs.  
- **Account changes** – They change your email or password, locking you out.  
- **Full account takeover** – If they change the email and password, they now control your account completely.  
- **Bigger damage** – If you’re an admin at work (like for a company system), the hacker might use your access to steal customer data, delete files, or take down the whole system.

So, the impact can range from *annoying* (someone liking posts as you on social media) to *disastrous* (losing money or exposing private data).
